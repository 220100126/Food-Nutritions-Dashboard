Fruites

Fruits are generally considered a great source of essential vitamins like vitamin C, vitamin A (beta-carotene), and potassium, alongside dietary fiber, providing a low-calorie, nutrient-dense food option

10%-20%

Grains

Grains are important sources of many nutrients, including complex carbohydrates, dietary fiber, several B vitamins, and minerals (iron, magnesium, and selenium).

65%

Bakery Items

When describing bakery items in terms of nutrition, key points to highlight include their often high carbohydrate content, typically from refined flour, alongside varying levels of added sugar and fat

21%

Milk

Milk is a nutrient-rich beverage considered a significant source of protein, particularly casein and whey, along with essential minerals like calcium, phosphorus, and potassium

4.9%

Meat

When describing meat in relation to nutrition facts, it's important to highlight its high protein content, significant source of essential vitamins like B12, and minerals like iron and zinc

1.2%

Fish

Fish is considered a highly nutritious food, providing a rich source of high-quality protein, essential omega-3 fatty acids, vitamins like vitamin D and B complex, and various minerals including selenium, iodine, phosphorus, calcium, iron, and zinc

10%-45%
